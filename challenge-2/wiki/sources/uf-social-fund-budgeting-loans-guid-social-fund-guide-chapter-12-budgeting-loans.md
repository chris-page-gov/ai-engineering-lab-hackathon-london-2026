OFFICIAL


      Social Fund Guide Chapter 12: Budgeting Loans
Version: March 2018
      NOTE: This chapter covers Budgeting Loans under the Social Fund. Budgeting Loans are being
      replaced by Budgeting Advances under Universal Credit. This guidance remains relevant for legacy
      Social Fund claims but should not be used for UC Budgeting Advance decisions — see the UC Decision
      Making Guide for that.



12.1 Introduction

Budgeting Loans are interest-free loans available to people who have been receiving a qualifying benefit
for at least 26 weeks. They are intended to help with intermittent expenses that are difficult to budget for.



12.2 Qualifying benefits

The applicant (or their partner) must have been in receipt of one or more of:

      • Income Support

      • income-based Jobseeker’s Allowance

      • income-related Employment and Support Allowance

      • Pension Credit

for a continuous period of at least 26 weeks at the date of application.



12.3 What Budgeting Loans can be used for

Loans may be awarded for:

      • Furniture and household equipment

      • Clothing and footwear

      • Rent in advance or removal expenses

      • Improvement, maintenance or security of the home

      • Travelling expenses

      • Expenses associated with seeking or starting work

      • Maternity expenses

      • Funeral expenses

      • HP or other debts for any of the above items
12.4 Minimum and maximum amounts

Minimum loan: £100

Maximum loan:

                Claimant type                                     Maximum

                Single claimant                                   £348

                Couple without children                           £464

                Claimant or couple with children                  £812

      [WARNING: These are the 2018 rates. They have NOT been uprated since 2018 and remain the same.
      However, always check the latest rates circular before making a decision.]



12.5 Factors affecting the loan amount

The amount awarded depends on:

      (a) The amount applied for

      (b) Whether the need can be met from other sources

      (c) Any outstanding Social Fund debt

      (d) The applicant’s ability to repay

Outstanding Social Fund debt is deducted from the maximum. For example, if a single claimant has an
outstanding Budgeting Loan of £100, the maximum available is £348 - £100 = £248.



12.6 Repayment

Repayment is by deduction from the qualifying benefit. The standard deduction rate is 12% of the
applicable amount (personal allowance plus premiums). A lower rate of 5% may be applied where
hardship would result.

Repayment period: up to 104 weeks.



12.7 Refusal

A Budgeting Loan application must be refused if:

      • The applicant has not been on a qualifying benefit for 26 weeks

      • Outstanding Social Fund debt equals or exceeds the maximum

      • The amount requested is below £100

      • The loan would take total Social Fund debt above the maximum
12.8 Reviews

Decisions on Budgeting Loans can be reviewed by a Social Fund Inspector. The applicant must request a
review within 28 days of the decision.
