OFFICIAL


                             Flexible Working Policy
Department for Work and Pensions | Effective: November 2023


1. Policy statement

DWP is committed to enabling flexible working arrangements that support both business needs and
employee wellbeing. All employees have the right to request flexible working from their first day of
employment.



2. Types of flexible working

The department supports the following arrangements:

      a) Part-time working

      b) Job sharing

      c) Compressed hours

      d) Flexitime

      e) Hybrid working (combination of office and home)

      f) Term-time working

      g) Annualised hours



3. Hybrid working

The department’s standard expectation is a minimum of 60% office attendance. This means for a full-time
employee working 5 days per week, at least 3 days should be spent in the office.

Some roles require higher office attendance due to the nature of the work (e.g., customer-facing roles,
roles requiring access to classified systems above OFFICIAL).

Managers may agree local arrangements that differ from the 60% standard where business needs allow,
but this must be documented and approved by the relevant Grade 6.



4. Requesting flexible working

To request a flexible working arrangement:

      1. Discuss informally with your line manager

      2. Submit a formal request through HR Online

      3. Your manager will respond within 28 days
      4. If approved, the arrangement will be confirmed in writing

      5. A trial period of 3 months applies to all new arrangements

Requests can only be refused on specific business grounds as set out in the Employment Rights Act 1996.



5. Core hours

Regardless of flexible working arrangements, all staff must be available during core hours: 10:00 to 12:00
and 14:00 to 16:00, Monday to Friday.



6. Equipment

Employees working from home are entitled to:

      • A department-issued laptop

      • A monitor (on request)

      • A keyboard and mouse (on request)

      • A desk assessment

The department will not provide office furniture for home use except where recommended by an
occupational health assessment.



7. Review

This policy was last reviewed in November 2023. Next review: November 2024.

Contact: flexible.working@dwp.gov.uk
