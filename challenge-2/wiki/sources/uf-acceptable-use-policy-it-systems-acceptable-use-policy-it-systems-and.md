OFFICIAL


         ACCEPTABLE USE POLICY IT SYSTEMS AND
                     EQUIPMENT
Department for Work and Pensions | Version: 4.0 | Date: June 2022 | Owner: Chief Digital and
Information Officer (CDIO)


1. Purpose

This policy sets out the rules for using DWP IT systems, equipment, and networks. It applies to all users of
DWP IT — including employees, contractors, agency staff, and secondees.

Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, termination of contract, or criminal
prosecution.



2. Acceptable Use

DWP IT systems are provided for business purposes. Limited personal use is permitted provided it does
not:

      • Interfere with work duties

      • Incur cost to the department

      • Bring the department into disrepute

      • Breach any other DWP policy



3. Email

3.1 Official email

All official correspondence must use your @dwp.gov.uk email address. The Department’s email system is
Microsoft Outlook, hosted on the DWP Exchange server.

      [NOTE TO SELF: Check if we've fully migrated to M365 yet. Some teams still on on-prem Exchange as
      of March 2022. Update before publication if migration complete.]


3.2 Email classification

All emails must carry the appropriate classification marking in the subject line.

OFFICIAL — no marking required (default)

OFFICIAL–SENSITIVE — mark in subject line

SECRET and above — must not be sent via email; use the secure network
3.3 Personal email

Personal webmail (Gmail, Hotmail, Yahoo, etc.) must not be accessed on DWP devices. DWP data must
never be sent to personal email addresses.



4. Internet

Internet access is provided through a filtered connection. The following categories of website are blocked:

      • Adult content

      • Gambling

      • Social media (see section 6)

      • Streaming media (except approved platforms)

      • File sharing and cloud storage (except approved services)

Attempts to bypass web filtering (using VPNs, proxy sites, Tor, etc.) are a serious breach of this policy and
will be treated as a security incident.



5. Software

Users must not install software on DWP devices without approval from the IT Service Desk. This includes:
desktop applications, browser extensions, mobile apps on department-issued phones.

Approved software is listed in the Software Catalogue on the IT intranet page.

      [The Software Catalogue was decommissioned in 2023 and replaced by the Digital Marketplace. This
      reference is outdated.]



6. Social Media

Access to social media platforms (Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, LinkedIn) is blocked on the DWP network
for most users. Exceptions may be granted for staff in Communications or Digital roles who require access
for official purposes.

See the separate Social Media Guidance for Staff for detailed guidance.



7. Remote Working

When working remotely, users must:

      • Connect via the DWP VPN (Cisco AnyConnect)

      • Not use public Wi-Fi without the VPN active

      • Ensure household members cannot see their screen

      • Lock devices when stepping away
      [NOTE: The VPN client is being replaced by Zscaler Internet Access (ZIA) — rollout began Q1 2023.
      Both are currently in use depending on the user’s device build. This section needs updating to reflect
      dual running.]



8. Monitoring

DWP reserves the right to monitor all use of its IT systems. This includes: email content and metadata,
internet browsing history, file access and transfers, login times and locations.

Monitoring is carried out in accordance with the Regulation of Investigatory Powers Act 2000 (RIPA) and
the Investigatory Powers Act 2016. Monitoring data is retained for 12 months.



9. Reporting

Any suspected breach of this policy or IT security incident must be reported immediately to:

IT Service Desk: 0800 XXX XXXX

Security Operations Centre: soc@dwp.gov.uk



10. Review

This policy is reviewed every two years.

Last review: June 2022 | Next review: June 2024
