# Right to Buy: Guidance for Tenants and Social Landlords

This guidance sets out the Right to Buy scheme as it applies in England. It covers eligibility, discount calculations, the application process, and landlord obligations. For the overarching housing policy framework, see the Housing Benefit and Housing Policy Overview ([DOC-HB-004](doc-hb-004-social-housing-frequently-asked-questions-gov-uk.md)).

## Eligibility

You may be eligible for the Right to Buy if you meet all of the following conditions:

- Your home is your only or main residence.
- You are a secure tenant of a local authority or a qualifying housing association.
- You must have been a public sector tenant for at least 3 years. Time spent as a tenant of any public sector landlord counts, including previous tenancies with different councils or housing associations.
- Your home is not in a designated rural area, a National Park, or an Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty where the Right to Buy has been suspended.
- Your home is not sheltered housing or housing adapted for persons with disabilities where specific exemptions apply.
- You are not subject to a court order for possession, bankruptcy, or anti-social behaviour.

If you are a joint tenant, only one of you needs to meet the tenancy length requirement, but all joint tenants must agree to the purchase.

## Discount Calculations

The discount you receive depends on the type of property and how long you have been a public sector tenant. Discounts are subject to a regional maximum cap.

### Discount Rates

| Property Type | Qualifying Tenancy Period | Discount (% of Market Value) | Additional Per Year | Maximum Discount |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| House | 3 to 5 years | 35% | +1% per additional year | 70% or regional cap |
| House | 5 to 10 years | 37% to 40% | +1% per additional year | 70% or regional cap |
| House | 10 years or more | 40%+ | +1% per additional year | 70% or regional cap |
| Flat | 3 to 5 years | 50% | +2% per additional year | 70% or regional cap |
| Flat | 5 to 10 years | 54% to 60% | +2% per additional year | 70% or regional cap |
| Flat | 10 years or more | 60%+ | +2% per additional year | 70% or regional cap |

The current regional maximum discount is 96,000 pounds outside London and 127,900 pounds in London. These caps are uprated annually in line with the Consumer Prices Index.

### Cost Floor Rule

If your landlord has spent money on building, acquiring, repairing, or maintaining your home within the 15 years before your application, the discount may be limited so that the purchase price does not fall below the amount spent. Your landlord must provide a breakdown of qualifying costs if the cost floor applies.

## Application Process

### Step 1: Submit Your Application

Complete the Right to Buy application form (RTB1) and submit it to your landlord. You can obtain the form from your landlord's housing office or download it from GOV.UK. Your landlord must acknowledge receipt within 4 weeks.

### Step 2: Landlord's Response

Your landlord must serve a notice (Section 124 notice) within 4 weeks of receiving your application if they admit the Right to Buy, or within 8 weeks if they deny it. If the Right to Buy is admitted, the notice will set out the terms of the proposed sale, including the market value, any structural defects, and service charge estimates for flats.

### Step 3: Valuation

The property will be valued by your landlord or an independent valuer at current market value on the assumption of a willing seller. If you disagree with the valuation, you have the right to request a determination by the District Valuer within 3 months of receiving the Section 124 notice.

### Step 4: Completion

Once terms are agreed, you have 12 months to complete the purchase. If you do not complete within this period, your landlord may serve a notice requiring completion within 56 days, after which the application lapses. You are responsible for your own legal costs and survey fees.

## Landlord Obligations

Social landlords have the following obligations under the Right to Buy scheme:

- Process applications promptly and within the statutory time limits set out above.
- Provide tenants with accurate information about their rights, the discount, and any costs that may apply.
- Not unreasonably delay or obstruct a valid Right to Buy application.
- Maintain the property in good repair until completion of the sale.
- Comply with the requirement to use Right to Buy receipts for replacement housing supply in accordance with Departmental directions.
- Report all Right to Buy sales to the Department on a quarterly basis using the prescribed return.

Failure to comply with these obligations may result in Departmental intervention, including the use of powers under section 164 of the Housing Act 1985 to determine the Right to Buy on the tenant's behalf.

## Repayment of Discount

If you sell your home within 5 years of purchasing it under the Right to Buy, you may be required to repay some or all of the discount. The repayment is calculated on a sliding scale:

- Sale within year 1: repay 100% of the discount
- Sale within year 2: repay 80% of the discount
- Sale within year 3: repay 60% of the discount
- Sale within year 4: repay 40% of the discount
- Sale within year 5: repay 20% of the discount

The repayment is based on the percentage of the discount relative to the current market value at the time of resale, not the original purchase price.

## Recent Changes

**October 2025 update:** The qualifying tenancy period for the Right to Buy has been increased from 3 years to 5 years for all new applications received on or after 1 April 2025. This change was introduced by the Social Housing (Regulation) Act 2025 and applies to all secure tenants in England. Applications submitted before 1 April 2025 are assessed under the previous 3-year qualifying period.

The regional maximum discount caps have also been frozen at their 2024/25 levels (96,000 pounds outside London and 127,900 pounds in London) and will not be uprated for the 2025/26 financial year.

These changes are intended to support the retention of social housing stock and ensure that the Right to Buy scheme operates sustainably alongside the Government's commitment to increase the supply of affordable housing.
