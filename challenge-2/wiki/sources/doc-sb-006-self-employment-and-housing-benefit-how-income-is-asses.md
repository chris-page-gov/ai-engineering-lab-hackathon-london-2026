# Self-employment and Housing Benefit: how income is assessed

## Overview

If you are self-employed and claiming Housing Benefit, your local authority must assess your income to determine the level of benefit you are entitled to receive. The assessment process for self-employed claimants differs significantly from that applied to employed earners, because self-employed individuals may have irregular income patterns and a wider range of permitted business expenses.

This guidance sets out how local authorities should calculate self-employment income for Housing Benefit purposes, including the application of the minimum income floor and the evidence that claimants are expected to provide.

## How self-employment income is calculated

Local authorities assess self-employment income on the basis of **net profit** rather than gross turnover. Net profit is calculated by deducting permitted business expenses from gross income over the relevant assessment period.

The assessment period is normally the most recent full trading year. Where the claimant has been self-employed for less than one year, the local authority should use the period from the start of trading to the date of the claim and annualise the result.

### Permitted expenses

Only expenses that are wholly and exclusively incurred for the purposes of the business may be deducted. These include:

- Rent for business premises (but not the domestic portion of a home used partly for business)
- Stock and materials
- Business insurance premiums
- Accountancy and professional fees
- Vehicle costs where the vehicle is used exclusively for business purposes
- Telephone and broadband costs attributable to business use

Capital expenditure, depreciation, and business entertaining are not permitted deductions.

## Worked example

The following example illustrates how Housing Benefit is calculated for a self-employed claimant:

| Step | Description | Amount |
|------|-------------|--------|
| 1 | Gross annual income | £24,000.00 |
| 2 | Less permitted expenses | £8,500.00 |
| 3 | Net profit (Step 1 minus Step 2) | £15,500.00 |
| 4 | Weekly equivalent (Step 3 / 52) | £298.08 |
| 5 | Applicable personal allowance | £77.00 |
| 6 | Excess income (Step 4 minus Step 5) | £221.08 |
| 7 | Taper rate applied | 65% |
| 8 | Weekly Housing Benefit reduction (Step 6 x 65%) | £143.70 |

In this example, the claimant's weekly Housing Benefit entitlement would be reduced by £143.70 from the maximum applicable rate. If the maximum weekly Housing Benefit for the claimant's circumstances were £270.00, they would receive £126.30 per week.

## Minimum income floor

Where a claimant has been self-employed for more than 12 months and is in gainful self-employment, the local authority must apply a **minimum income floor** (MIF). The MIF assumes that the claimant is earning at least the equivalent of the National Minimum Wage for the number of hours they would be expected to work (typically 35 hours per week for a single claimant without caring responsibilities).

If the claimant's actual self-employment income falls below the MIF, the higher figure is used for the purposes of calculating Housing Benefit. The MIF does not apply during the first 12 months of a new business (the "start-up period").

## Evidence requirements

Claimants must provide evidence of their self-employment income and expenses. Acceptable evidence includes:

- Self-assessment tax returns for the most recent complete tax year
- Business accounts prepared by a qualified accountant
- Business bank statements covering the assessment period
- Invoices, receipts, and records of expenses

Local authorities may request additional evidence where the information provided is incomplete or inconsistent. Where a claimant fails to provide adequate evidence, the authority may make a determination based on available information, including the application of the minimum income floor.
