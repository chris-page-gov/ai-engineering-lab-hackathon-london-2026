OFFICIAL


                     Recruitment and Selection Policy
Department for Work and Pensions | December 2023

Document control: Version 6.0 | Owner: Resourcing Team, HR Directorate | Review date:
December 2025


1. Principles

All recruitment to DWP must be conducted in accordance with the Civil Service Commission’s Recruitment
Principles. Appointment must be on merit on the basis of fair and open competition.

Exceptions to fair and open competition include:

      • Managed moves within the Civil Service

      • Loans and secondments

      • Redeployment (including as a reasonable adjustment)

      • Permitted exceptions as defined by the Civil Service Commission



2. Approval to Recruit

Before any vacancy can be advertised, approval must be obtained from the Workforce Planning team.

      Grade of vacancy           Approver                     Additional requirements

      AA to EO                   Grade 7 (budget holder)      Confirm budget allocation

      HEO to SEO                 Grade 6                      Business case (1 page)

      Grade 7                    SCS1 (Director)              Business case + WFP approval

      Grade 6                    SCS2 (Director General)      Business case + ExCo notification

      SCS                        Permanent Secretary          Business case + Cabinet Office approval

      Note: These approval levels differ from the procurement approval matrix (see Spending Controls
      Guidance). Recruitment costs should be considered separately from procurement — the Procurement
      Thresholds do not apply to salary commitments.



3. Advertising

Vacancies must be advertised on Civil Service Jobs (www.civilservicejobs.service.gov.uk) for a minimum
of: 5 working days for internal competitions; 10 working days for external competitions.

The use of recruitment agencies requires Commercial Directorate approval regardless of cost, as
recruitment agency spend falls under the consultancy spending control.
4. Selection Methods

All selection processes must include at least: a sift stage (application review) and an interview.

Additional assessment methods may include: written tests or work samples, presentations, assessment
centres (typically Grade 7 and above), psychometric tests (SCS only, using Civil Service approved tools).



5. Interview Panels

      Grade of vacancy           Panel size      Requirements

      AA to EO                   2               At least one trained interviewer

      HEO to SEO                 2               Panel chair at least one grade above vacancy

      Grade 7 to Grade 6         3               Panel chair at SCS level; at least one external panel member for Grade 6

      SCS                        3-4             Civil Service Commissioner oversight; independent panel member required

All panel members must have completed unconscious bias training within the last 2 years.



6. Pre-employment Checks

The following checks are mandatory before appointment: identity verification, right to work in the UK,
criminal record check (DBS Basic as minimum; enhanced for specific roles), employment history
verification, health declaration, qualification verification.

      Role type                            Minimum clearance                        Processing time

      Most DWP roles                       Baseline Personnel Security Standard 1-2
                                                                                (BPSS)
                                                                                    weeks

      Roles accessing sensitive systems    Security Check (SC)                      6-8 weeks

      Roles accessing TOP SECRET materialDeveloped Vetting (DV)                     6-9 months




7. Offers and Onboarding

Conditional offers may be made subject to pre-employment checks. Unconditional offers must not be
made until all checks are complete.

Starting salary is normally at the minimum of the pay band. Starting above the minimum requires Grade 6
approval and must be justified by market data or the candidate’s experience.



8. Record Retention

Recruitment records must be retained for 12 months after the vacancy closes (for unsuccessful
candidates) or for the duration of employment plus 6 years (for successful candidates).

Psychometric test results are retained for 2 years only.
9. Monitoring

The Resourcing Team reports quarterly on: time to hire (target: 12 weeks from approval to start date),
diversity of applicants, shortlisted candidates, and appointees, cost per hire, hiring manager satisfaction.



10. Complaints

Candidates who believe the recruitment process was unfair may complain to: the Resourcing Team (for
process complaints) or the Civil Service Commission (for complaints about the merit principle).

Contact: recruitment@dwp.gov.uk

Civil Service Commission: https://civilservicecommission.independent.gov.uk
