COUNCIL TAX REDUCTION SCHEMES: REGULATORY FRAMEWORK AND LOCAL AUTHORITY OBLIGATIONS

Document ID: DOC-HB-003
Published: March 2024
Department: Department for Levelling Up, Housing and Communities
Status: Current
Audience: Local authority revenues and benefits officers


1. Introduction and statutory basis

1.1 This document provides a summary of the regulatory framework governing Council Tax Reduction (CTR) schemes in England. CTR replaced the national Council Tax Benefit scheme with effect from 1 April 2013 pursuant to the provisions of the Local Government Finance Act 2012. Each billing authority in England is required under section 13A(2) of the Local Government Finance Act 1992 (as amended) to make a scheme specifying the reductions to council tax liability to which persons may be entitled.

1.2 The Secretary of State has prescribed certain requirements with which all local CTR schemes must comply. These prescribed requirements are set out in The Council Tax Reduction Schemes (Prescribed Requirements) (England) Regulations 2012 (SI 2012/2885), as subsequently amended by The Council Tax Reduction Schemes (Prescribed Requirements) (England) (Amendment) Regulations 2013 (SI 2013/3181), The Council Tax Reduction Schemes (Prescribed Requirements) (England) (Amendment) Regulations 2014 (SI 2014/3312), and subsequent annual amendment regulations through to the most recent amending instrument.

1.3 The prescribed requirements regulations establish the framework within which billing authorities must operate. They specify the classes of person who must be included in the scheme, the maximum period for which a reduction may be backdated, and the procedural requirements for applications and determinations. Authorities retain discretion in respect of the level of reduction offered to working-age claimants, subject to the requirement that pensioner-age claimants must receive reductions calculated in accordance with the prescribed scheme set out in Schedule 1 to SI 2012/2885.


2. Scope of local authority discretion

2.1 For working-age applicants, each billing authority may design its own scheme, subject to the prescribed requirements. This means that the level of council tax reduction available to a working-age person varies between different local authority areas. Some authorities have maintained schemes that broadly replicate the former national Council Tax Benefit scheme, providing up to 100 per cent reduction. Other authorities have introduced minimum payments, income tapers, or capital limits that differ from the previous national scheme.

2.2 Billing authorities must consult with precepting authorities and publish a draft scheme before adoption. Any revision to an existing scheme for a financial year must be agreed by a resolution of the full council before 11 March in the preceding financial year. Where an authority fails to agree a revised scheme by that date, the scheme from the previous year continues in effect by default.

2.3 The interaction between CTR schemes and Housing Benefit entitlement (see [DOC-HB-001](doc-hb-001-housing-benefit-check-if-you-re-eligible-gov-uk.md) for Housing Benefit eligibility criteria) requires careful consideration. A person who receives Housing Benefit or Universal Credit housing costs element may also be eligible for CTR. The CTR assessment takes into account the applicant's income, capital, and household composition, and local schemes may apply different disregards and premiums to those used in the Housing Benefit calculation.


3. Prescribed requirements for pensioner-age applicants

3.1 The prescribed requirements mandate that all CTR schemes must include provision for persons who have attained the qualifying age for state pension credit. The reduction available to pensioner-age applicants is calculated in accordance with Schedule 1 to SI 2012/2885 and is not subject to local variation. This ensures that pensioner-age applicants receive the same level of protection regardless of which billing authority area they reside in.

3.1.1 The applicable amount for a pensioner-age single claimant is determined by reference to the amounts set out in Schedule 2 to the prescribed requirements regulations. These amounts are uprated annually by statutory instrument.

3.1.2 For pensioner-age couples, the applicable amount includes the couple rate personal allowance together with any relevant premiums, including the severe disability premium where the qualifying conditions are met. The calculation follows the methodology previously used under the national Council Tax Benefit scheme.

3.1.3 Where a pensioner-age applicant has income that exceeds the applicable amount, the excess income is subject to a taper of 20 per cent. That is, for every pound by which the applicant's weekly income exceeds the applicable amount, the weekly CTR entitlement is reduced by 20 pence.


4. Procedural requirements

4.1 Applications for CTR must be made to the billing authority in whose area the dwelling is situated. Authorities must accept applications made in writing, electronically, or by telephone where appropriate arrangements are in place. An authority may require an applicant to provide such information and evidence as it reasonably requires to determine the application.

4.1.1 Where an application is defective by reason of it being made on the incorrect form or omitting material information, the authority must afford the applicant an opportunity to rectify the deficiency. The authority may not refuse the application solely on the ground that the prescribed form was not used if the applicant has provided sufficient information for the authority to determine entitlement.

4.2 Billing authorities must make a determination on a CTR application as soon as reasonably practicable after receiving all necessary information and evidence. The authority must give written notice of its determination, including where the application is refused the reasons for refusal and the applicant's right of appeal to the Valuation Tribunal for England under section 16 of the Local Government Finance Act 1992.

4.3 Any person aggrieved by a decision of a billing authority regarding CTR entitlement may appeal to the Valuation Tribunal for England. Before making an appeal, the person should first request that the authority reconsider the decision under the authority's own review procedure. If the dispute is not resolved, an appeal may be made within two months of notification of the authority's decision on review.


5. Fraud and compliance

5.1 Council Tax Reduction is subject to the same fraud investigation powers as apply to Housing Benefit and Council Tax Benefit under the Social Security Administration Act 1992 and the Council Tax Reduction Schemes (Detection of Fraud and Enforcement) (England) Regulations 2013 (SI 2013/501). Billing authorities have the power to require information from applicants and third parties for the purpose of investigating potential fraud.

5.1.1 Where an authority determines that a person has received CTR to which they were not entitled as a result of fraud, the authority may impose a civil penalty of 50 per cent of the excess reduction, subject to a minimum of 100 pounds and a maximum of 1,000 pounds. The authority must give the person written notice of the penalty and the right to appeal to the Valuation Tribunal.
