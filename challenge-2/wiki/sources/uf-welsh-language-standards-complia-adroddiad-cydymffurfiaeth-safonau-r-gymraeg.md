1. Cyflwyniad / Introduction

This report sets out DWP’s compliance with the Welsh Language Standards during the reporting period 1
April 2022 to 31 March 2023. It is published in accordance with the Welsh Language (Wales) Measure
2011 and the compliance notice issued to DWP by the Welsh Language Commissioner.

Ebrill 2022 i 31 Mawrth 2023.

3. Areas of non-compliance

3.1 Telephone services

Standard 8 requires DWP to offer a Welsh language option on all telephone helplines serving Wales.
During the reporting period, 4 helplines did not provide this option:

      • Universal Credit helpline (Welsh callers routed to English-speaking agents)

      • Disability benefits helpline

      • Carer’s Allowance helpline

      • Debt Management helpline
Remedial action: Welsh language advisers are being recruited. Target for full compliance: September
2023.

      [NOTE: As of the date of publication (March 2024), the UC helpline and Debt Management helpline
      remain non-compliant. The September 2023 target was missed.]


3.2 Digital services

Standard 36 requires Welsh language versions of all new and redesigned digital services serving Welsh
users. The following services were launched without Welsh language versions during the reporting period:

      • UC journal messaging feature (launched June 2022)

      • PIP online claim tracking tool (launched November 2022)

Both services now have Welsh language versions (delivered January and March 2023 respectively).


3.3 Signage

Standard 61 requires bilingual signage at all DWP sites in Wales. An audit in February 2023 found that 3 of
47 Jobcentre Plus sites in Wales had English-only signage: Rhyl Jobcentre, Bangor Jobcentre,
Aberystwyth Jobcentre.

All three sites were rectified by April 2023.



4. Complaints

During the reporting period, DWP received 23 complaints related to Welsh language provision. Of these:

      • 14 related to telephone services (inability to conduct business in Welsh)

      • 5 related to correspondence received in English only

      • 3 related to digital services

      • 1 related to a Jobcentre Plus site

All complaints were resolved within the 20 working day target. The Welsh Language Commissioner was
notified of 2 complaints that required formal investigation.



5. Welsh language skills

As at 31 March 2023, DWP employed 3,247 staff in Wales, of whom:

      • 412 (12.7%) self-reported as Welsh speakers

      • 186 (5.7%) were in designated Welsh essential posts

      • 89 (2.7%) were in designated Welsh desirable posts

The department offers Welsh language learning through the National Centre for Learning Welsh. During
the reporting period, 34 staff enrolled in Welsh language courses.

7. Cynlluniau ar gyfer 2023-24 / Plans for 2023-24

      • Achieve full compliance with telephone service standards by December 2023

      • Launch Welsh language chatbot pilot on GOV.UK (subject to GOV.UK App development)

      • Increase number of designated Welsh essential posts by 15%

      • Implement simultaneous publication of all new policy documents in Welsh and English

      [Several of these targets for 2023-24 have since passed their deadlines. An updated compliance report
      for 2023-24 is expected by March 2025 but has not yet been published.]
