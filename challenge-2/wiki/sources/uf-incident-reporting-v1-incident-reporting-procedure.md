**INCIDENT REPORTING PROCEDURE**

Version 1.0 \| January 2023

Department for Work and Pensions — Health and Safety Team

Purpose

This procedure describes how to report workplace incidents, near misses, and hazards. It applies to all DWP premises including Jobcentres, service centres, and corporate offices.

Definitions

**Incident:** An event that results in injury, ill health, or damage to property.

**Near miss:** An event that could have resulted in an incident but did not.

**Hazard:** A condition or situation with the potential to cause harm.

**RIDDOR:** Reporting of Injuries, Diseases and Dangerous Occurrences Regulations 2013.

Reporting process

All incidents must be reported on the same day they occur using the online reporting form (accessible from the H&S intranet page).

|  |  |  |
|----|----|----|
| **Severity** | **Action** | **Timeframe** |
| Minor (first aid only) | Complete online form | Same day |
| Moderate (medical treatment) | Complete online form + notify line manager | Within 2 hours |
| Major (hospital admission) | Complete online form + notify H&S team by phone | Immediately |
| Fatal or specified injury | As above + RIDDOR report | Immediately |

Investigation

All moderate, major, and RIDDOR-reportable incidents will be investigated by the Health and Safety team. Minor incidents are investigated locally by the line manager.

Investigation reports must be completed within:

- Minor: 5 working days

- Moderate: 10 working days

- Major/RIDDOR: 15 working days

Record keeping

All incident records must be retained for a minimum of 3 years. RIDDOR records must be retained for a minimum of 3 years from the date of the incident (note: this aligns with the statutory requirement but some legal advisors recommend 6 years).

Contact

H&S Team: health.safety@dwp.gov.uk

Emergency: 999

DWP Security Control Room: 0800 XXX XXXX (24/7)
