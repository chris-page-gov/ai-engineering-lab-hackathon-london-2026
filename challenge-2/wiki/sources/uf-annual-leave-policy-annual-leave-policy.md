**Annual Leave Policy**

Department for Work and Pensions \| April 2024

1\. Entitlement

Annual leave entitlement depends on length of service and grade:

|  |  |  |  |  |  |
|----|----|----|----|----|----|
| **Grade** | **Under 1 year** | **1-2 years** | **2-5 years** | **5-10 years** | **10+ years** |
| AA/AO | 23 days | 23 days | 24 days | 25 days | 30 days |
| EO | 23 days | 23 days | 24 days | 25 days | 30 days |
| HEO/SEO | 25 days | 25 days | 25 days | 27 days | 30 days |
| Grade 7/6 | 25 days | 25 days | 27 days | 27 days | 30 days |
| SCS | 30 days | 30 days | 30 days | 30 days | 30 days |

In addition, all staff receive 8 public holidays and 1 privilege day (the King’s Birthday).

2\. Carry over

Up to 5 days of unused annual leave may be carried over to the following leave year. Leave carried over must be taken by 30 June.

In exceptional circumstances, additional carry over may be approved by a Grade 6.

3\. Booking leave

Leave must be requested through the department’s HR system. Line managers should respond to leave requests within 3 working days.

Leave should not normally be refused unless there is a genuine business need. Where leave is refused, the manager must explain the reason in writing.

4\. Half days

Leave may be taken in half-day increments. A half day is defined as half the employee’s contracted daily hours.

5\. Unpaid leave

Employees may request unpaid leave for reasons not covered by other leave policies. Unpaid leave of up to 5 days may be approved by a line manager. Longer periods require Grade 6 approval.

6\. Leave year

The leave year runs from 1 April to 31 March.

7\. Leaving the department

When an employee leaves DWP, their leave entitlement is calculated pro rata. Any excess leave taken will be deducted from final salary. Any leave owed will be paid.

Contact: hr.leave@dwp.gov.uk
