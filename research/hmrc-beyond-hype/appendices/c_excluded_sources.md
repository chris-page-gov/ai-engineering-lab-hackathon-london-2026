# Appendix C: Excluded Or Limited Sources

| Source or source class | Status | Reason | Impact on talk |
|---|---|---|---|
| Raw Codex transcripts outside the public postmortem | Excluded from publication claims | They may contain local paths, private context, or unpublished working material. | Use the public postmortem and committed reports instead. |
| Unverified meeting-transcript snippets naming Alicia/Aesha Patel | Limited | The user's prep says the transcript has a name error. | Use only as context for audience needs and event shape, not as a factual public citation. |
| Vendor marketing pages without dates or concrete product documentation | Excluded for substantive claims | Marketing claims do not independently prove capability, quality, productivity, or safety. | Use official product pages only for timeline/capability framing. |
| News articles summarising product launches | Mostly excluded | Primary vendor announcements were available. | Use primary sources instead. |
| Product comparison blogs | Excluded | Usually not stable, independent, or sufficiently methodological. | Do not use for "which tool is best" claims. |
| SWE-bench Live or equivalent live-contamination benchmark references not verified in this pass | Excluded as primary source | The pack could not verify a stable primary source within the research pass. | Discuss contamination-resistant evaluation as a principle, supported by SWE-bench and OpenAI benchmark caveats. |
| Unpublished local benchmark raw prompts and raw answers | Excluded from publication | Committed reports intentionally keep raw prompts and answer text outside Git. | Cite public-safe score summaries only. |
| Anecdotal productivity multipliers | Excluded | The talk needs defensible evidence, not unsupported speed claims. | Use conditional claims grounded in Peng et al., METR, DORA, and repo-local chronology. |
| Real HMRC data assumptions | Excluded | The repo uses synthetic Challenge 2 fixture data. | The talk must say real data requires approved handling and assurance. |
| Production-readiness inference from test passing | Excluded | Tests validate bounded behaviours, not all security, data-protection, policy, or operational risks. | Treat validation as prototype evidence only. |
